# V6.6.4 Incident Analysis

## Incident A: Laos and Thailand were not retained as SEA

The group name supplied explicit `TH` and `LA` evidence through country names and flags. The country matcher and flag matcher each correctly collapsed those countries into `SEA`. When both matcher results were combined, however, the source was rewritten as plain `country_keyword_and_flag`, losing the `_same_business_region` marker.

The downstream logic inspected the raw keyword hits, saw two country codes, and treated the row as an unresolved multi-region case. With no About location or geocoder result, the final fallback refused to retain the already-computed `SEA` value because the source no longer carried `_same_business_region`.

V6.6.4 preserves the marker during evidence combination and treats a resolved same-business-region set as a completed result rather than a cross-region conflict.

## Incident B: GAG matched gags and gagged

The old matcher normalized group names into a compact string and used unrestricted `includes()` matching. Because `gags`, `gagged`, and `9gag` contain the substring `gag`, they were accepted as strong title matches.

V6.6.4 applies token boundaries to short Latin aliases. These ordinary words and unrelated names no longer match `GAG`.

## Incident C: GAG2 and GAG 2 were attributed to GAG

Two defects combined:

1. compact substring matching treated `gag2` as containing `gag`;
2. word matching treated `GAG` in `GAG 2` as a standalone word;
3. sibling exclusion contained the canonical title `Grow a Garden 2` but did not automatically contain sibling aliases and configured variants such as `GAG2`.

V6.6.4 recognizes `GAG2` and `GAG 2` as equivalent forms of the longer alias, adds sibling aliases and variants to negative evidence, and prevents the shorter `GAG` alias from matching a numeric continuation.

## Scope

These were deterministic Skill defects in title and region classification. They were not caused by Facebook extraction, CDP login, checkpoint corruption, GeoNames availability, or the multi-game `group_url + game_name` output rule.
